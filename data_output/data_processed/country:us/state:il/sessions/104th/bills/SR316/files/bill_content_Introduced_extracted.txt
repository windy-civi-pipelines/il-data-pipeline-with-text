5and 6    WHEREAS, Katie Huther was born on August 17, 1934; she was 7raised in Utica, New York; she earned a degree in American 8studies at the University of Rochester in New York; she 9married John Huther, and together they relocated to California 10and Washington through the U.S. Navy and pursued jobs and 11educational pursuits in New York, Michigan, and North Carolina 12before settling in Springfield, Illinois in 1973; they later 13relocated to Seattle, Washington in 2015; and 14    WHEREAS, Katie Huther held a variety of jobs, culminating 15in her serving as the assistant for community relations at 16Springfield Public Schools for ten years; and 17    WHEREAS, Katie Huther's strong commitment to civic 18engagement led to her involvement with numerous organizations, 19including the Illinois Environmental Council, the League of 20Women Voters, and the PTA; in her later years, she served on 21the boards of the Lincoln Memorial Garden, the Illinois 22Symphony Orchestra, the World Affairs Council of Central   SR0316- 2 -LRB104 13483 ECR 25923 r1Illinois, and others; and 2    WHEREAS, Katie Huther enjoyed being social with her 3gourmet dinner groups, the Anti-Rust Club, her book club, and 4her bridge club; she also loved traveling with her husband and 5other family members to her family's old homestead in 6Woodgate, New York each summer; and 7    WHEREAS, Katie Huther always welcomed friends and family 8with food and entertainment, leaving them with lasting 9memories; and 10    WHEREAS, Katie Huther is survived by her husband, John; 11her children, Jeff, Eric, and Melissa; her sister, Jane; and 12six grandchildren; therefore, be it 13    RESOLVED, BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTH GENERAL 14ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ILLINOIS, that we mourn the passing of 15Catherine "Katie" Kronmiller Huther and extend our sincere 16condolences to her family, friends, and all who knew and loved 17her; and be it further 18    RESOLVED, That a suitable copy of this resolution be 19presented to the family of Katie Huther as an expression of our 20deepest sympathy.
