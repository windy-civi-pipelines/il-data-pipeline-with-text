Full Text of SR0051 ILGA.GOV SR0051 - 104th General Assembly SR0051LRB104 10389 ECR 20464 r1SENATE RESOLUTION 2    WHEREAS, The members of the Illinois Senate are saddened 3to learn of the death of Ralph Vernon Froehling of Canton, who 4passed away on January 9, 2025; and 5    WHEREAS, Ralph Froehling, the son of Arthur and Julia 6(Wilkenning) Froehling, was born in Harvey on October 5, 1928; 7he graduated from Cissna Park High School; he enlisted in the 8U.S. Army, serving from 1946 to 1948; with funding from the 9G.I. Bill, he attended the University of Illinois and was 10accepted into the University of Illinois Law School, earning 11his Juris Doctor in 1952; he married Jean Millington at Wesley 12United Church in Canton on January 27, 1952; he was recalled to 13active duty during the Korean War and served in the Advocate 14General Department of the Army; and 15    WHEREAS, Ralph Froehling began his legal practice in Oak 16Park and then practiced law in Galesburg; he joined the 17Chiperfield Law Firm in Canton in 1956; attorneys Ron Weber 18and Nancy Schell became partners years later, and the firm is 19currently known as Froehling, Weber & Schell; and 20    WHEREAS, Ralph Froehling was recognized as Senior 21Counselor by the Illinois Bar Association in 2002; and   SR0051- 2 -LRB104 10389 ECR 20464 r1    WHEREAS, Ralph Froehling was an active member of his 2community, serving in the Kiwanis Club of Canton and the 3Canton Rotary Club as well as on the Board of the 4Parlin-Ingersoll Library Board of Trustees and with other 5local organizations; and 6    WHEREAS, Ralph Froehling was preceded in death by his 7brother, Harold, and his sister, Alice; and 8    WHEREAS, Ralph Froehling is survived by his wife, Jean; 9his sons, Alan Froehling, John Froehling, and David Froehling; 10his daughter, Lynn (Brian) Martie; eight grandchildren; 12 11great-grandchildren; and numerous nieces, nephews, 12great-nieces, and great-nephews; therefore, be it 13    RESOLVED, BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTH GENERAL 14ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ILLINOIS, that we mourn the passing of 15Ralph Vernon Froehling and extend our sincere condolences to 16his family, friends, and all who knew and loved him; and be it 17further 18    RESOLVED, That a suitable copy of this resolution be 19presented to the family of Ralph Froehling as an expression of 20our deepest sympathy.
