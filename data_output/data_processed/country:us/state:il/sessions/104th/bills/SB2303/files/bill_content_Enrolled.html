1    AN ACT concerning regulation.
 
2    Be it enacted by the People of the State of Illinois, 
3represented in the General Assembly:
 
4    Section 5. The Fire Sprinkler Contractor Licensing Act is 
5amended by changing Section 62 as follows:
 
6    (225 ILCS 317/62)
7    Sec. 62. Unlicensed practice; violation; civil penalty. 
8    (a) Any person, entity, or business that offers fire 
9sprinkler contractor services under this Act without being 
10licensed or exempt under this Act shall, in addition to any 
11other penalty provided by law, pay a civil penalty, which 
12shall be deposited into the Fire Prevention Fund, in an amount 
13not to exceed $10,000 for each offense, as determined by the 
14Office. If any person, entity, or business commits a second 
15offense within 24 months, the civil penalty shall be no less 
16than $10,000, which shall be deposited into the Fire 
17Prevention Fund. If any person, entity, or business commits 
18more than 2 offenses within 24 months, the civil penalty shall 
19be no less than $25,000, which shall be deposited into the Fire 
20Prevention Fund. The civil penalty shall be assessed by the 
21Office after a hearing is held in accordance with the 
22provisions of this Act regarding the provision of a hearing 
23for the discipline of a licensee.

1    (b) Use of the title "fire sprinkler inspector" is limited 
2to those individuals licensed under this Act. Any person who 
3practices, offers to practice, attempts to practice, or holds 
4himself or herself out to practice as a fire sprinkler 
5inspector without being licensed or exempt under this Act 
6shall, in addition to any other penalty provided by law, pay a 
7civil penalty, which shall be deposited into the Fire 
8Prevention Fund, in an amount not to exceed $10,000 for each 
9offense, as determined by the Office. If any person commits a 
10second offense within 24 months, the civil penalty shall be no 
11less than $10,000, which shall be deposited into the Fire 
12Prevention Fund. If any person commits more than 2 offenses 
13within 24 months, the civil penalty shall be no less than 
14$25,000, which shall be deposited into the Fire Prevention 
15Fund. The civil penalty shall be assessed by the Office after a 
16hearing is held in accordance with the provisions of this Act 
17regarding the provision of a hearing for the discipline of a 
18licensee.
19    (c) The Office may investigate any actual, alleged, or 
20suspected unlicensed activity.
21    (d) The civil penalty shall be paid within 60 days after 
22the effective date of the order imposing the civil penalty. 
23The order shall constitute a final judgment and may be filed 
24and execution had thereon in the same manner as any judgment 
25from any court of record. 
26(Source: P.A. 102-612, eff. 8-27-21.)
