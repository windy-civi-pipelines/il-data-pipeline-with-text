SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION

 
2    WHEREAS, It is highly fitting that the Illinois General 
3Assembly pays honor and respect to those individuals who have 
4given their lives in service to their community; and
 
5    WHEREAS, Deputy Sheriff Christina Musil, a beacon of joy 
6and resilience, was born to Philip and Diane Caporaso on 
7August 17, 1988; and
 
8    WHEREAS, Deputy Musil dedicated her time to bettering the 
9communities of DeKalb County as a deputy of the DeKalb County 
10Sheriff's Office; and
 
11    WHEREAS, Deputy Musil served in the U.S. Army National 
12Guard, including completing a tour of duty in Afghanistan from 
132008 to 2009; and
 
14    WHEREAS, Deputy Musil was a passionate fan of the New York 
15Yankees and the New Jersey Devils, but her truest enthusiasm 
16as a fan was reserved for watching her children compete in 
17their youth sport organizations; and
 
18    WHEREAS, Deputy Musil was killed in the line of duty on 
19March 29, 2024; and

1    WHEREAS, At the time of her passing, Deputy Musil was 
2survived by her children, Zoe, Genevieve, and Cillin; her 
3parents; her siblings, Bridgette Caporaso and Anthony 
4Caporaso; and additional family members and friends; and
 
5    WHEREAS, Deputy Musil will be remembered as an incredible 
6woman with a magnetic personality, a ready laugh, and a 
7selfless spirit; therefore, be it
 
8    RESOLVED, BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTH GENERAL 
9ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ILLINOIS, THE HOUSE OF 
10REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING HEREIN, that we designate State 
11Route 23 from Perry Road to McGirr Road as the "Deputy Sheriff 
12Christina Musil Memorial Highway"; and be it further
 
13    RESOLVED, That the Illinois Department of Transportation 
14is requested to erect at suitable locations, consistent with 
15State and federal regulations, appropriate plaques or signs 
16giving notice of the name "Deputy Sheriff Christina Musil 
17Memorial Highway"; and be it further
 
18    RESOLVED, That suitable copies of this resolution be 
19presented to the family of Deputy Sheriff Christina Musil and 
20the Secretary of the Illinois Department of Transportation.
