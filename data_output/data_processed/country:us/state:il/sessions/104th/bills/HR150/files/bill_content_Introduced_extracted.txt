and 17    WHEREAS, Speaker Welch's commitment to workers' rights is 18deeply personal; growing up in the Chicago suburbs, he saw 19firsthand how his father's union job was lost when it moved to 20a right-to-work state; this experience fueled his efforts as 21an elected official to defend collective bargaining rights, 22ultimately leading to the adoption of the Workers' Rights   HR0150- 2 -LRB104 12486 MST 22840 r1Amendment in the Illinois State Constitution; and 2    WHEREAS, Education has also been a key priority for 3Speaker Welch; before serving in the legislature, he spent 12 4years on the Proviso Township High School Board of Education, 5including a decade as board chair;
