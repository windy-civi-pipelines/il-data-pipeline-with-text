Full Text of SB2092 ILGA.GOV SB2092 - 104th General Assembly 104TH GENERAL ASSEMBLY State of Illinois 2025 and 2026SB2092 Introduced 2/6/2025, by Sen. Erica Harriss SYNOPSIS AS INTRODUCED: 740 ILCS 21/140 new    Amends the Stalking No Contact Order. Provides that an order issued under the Act may be expunged if the petitioner who sought the emergency or plenary order agrees that the order was issued in error as a mistake in fact and that the court makes a similar finding. Requires that the clerk of the court to immediately file a certified copy of the expungement order with the Illinois State Police and that all records related to an emergency order required to be expunged no later than 3 business days after the court issues the expungement order.LRB104 08527 JRC 18579 b  A BILL FOR SB2092LRB104 08527 JRC 18579 b1    AN ACT concerning civil law. 2    Be it enacted by the People of the State of Illinois, 3represented in the General Assembly: 4    Section 5. The Stalking No Contact Order Act is amended by 5adding Section 140 as follows: 6    (740 ILCS 21/140 new)7    Sec. 140. Expungement for mistake of fact.8    (a) Upon the petition of a respondent subject to an order 9issued under this Act, the court shall order that all records 10related to the emergency or plenary order be expunged from the 11court's records and from the Law Enforcement Agencies Data 12System maintained by the Illinois State Police if the 13petitioner who sought the emergency or plenary order agrees 14that the order was issued in error as a mistake in fact and 15that the court makes a similar finding.16    (b) The clerk of the court shall immediately file a 17certified copy of the expungement order with the Illinois 18State Police.19    (c) All records related to an emergency order required to 20be expunged under this Section shall be expunged no later than 213 business days after the court issues the expungement order.
