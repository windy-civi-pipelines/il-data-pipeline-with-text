SENATE RESOLUTION

 
2    WHEREAS, The members of the Illinois Senate are saddened 
3to learn of the death of Arthur Earl Spiegel of Springfield, 
4who passed away on March 4, 2025; and
 
5    WHEREAS, Arthur Spiegel, the son of Rev. Dr. Clarence and 
6Gertrude Spiegel, was born in Akron, Ohio on January 23, 1929; 
7he and his family moved to Springfield in 1938; he graduated 
8from Trinity Lutheran School in 1942 and Lanphier High School 
9(LHS) in 1946, and he was later inducted into the LHS Hall of 
10Fame in 2019; he enlisted in the U.S. Navy in October 1948; he 
11was later drafted into the U.S. Army, and he was honorably 
12discharged in December 1952; after briefly attending the 
13school before his military service, he returned to Illinois 
14State University in 1953, receiving his Bachelor of Science in 
15Education in January 1956 and his Master of Science in Public 
16School Administration in 1961; and
 
17    WHEREAS, Arthur Spiegel began his teaching career at 
18Springfield High School; he later worked as a teacher and 
19principal at Sherman Elementary School during the 1965-1966 
20school year; he served as principal at Athens High School from 
211967 to 1979 and as superintendent of Athens Community Unit 
22School District 213 from 1969 to 1971; during these years, he 
23served on the board of directors of the Illinois State

1University Alumni Association; he began working for the 
2Illinois Secretary of State in 1971, remaining in that office 
3for 12 years; he then served as utility property manager at 
4City Water, Light & Power (CWLP) for eight years, retiring in 
51991; and
 
6    WHEREAS, Arthur Spiegel was elected as charter president 
7of the Kiwanis Club, Springfield North in 1965 and lieutenant 
8governor of Division 11, Illinois-Eastern Iowa District of 
9Kiwanis International in 1970; he also served as a counselor 
10to the Kiwanis Lanphier High School Key Club; he served as 
11scoutmaster of Boy Scout Troop 47, Trinity Lutheran Church, 
12and he was also a member of the Land of Lincoln Scout Council, 
13serving as chairman of the School Night for Cubing; he later 
14served as cub scoutmaster for Troop 20, Our Savior's Lutheran 
15Church; he was a member of the Land of Lincoln Barbershop Choir 
16and the Illinois Symphony Choir; and
 
17    WHEREAS, Arthur Spiegel enjoyed playing the piano at 
18nursing homes, high-rise apartment buildings, church 
19functions, and Kiwanis Clubs in the Springfield area; in 
20retirement, he played the piano at Concordia Village, where he 
21also served as charter president of the Independent Resident 
22Council; and
 
23    WHEREAS, Arthur Spiegel committed to his faith at an early
