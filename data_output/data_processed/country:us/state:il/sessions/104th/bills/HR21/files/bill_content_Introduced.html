6    (House Rule 42)
7    42. Consent Calendar. 
8    (a) The Clerk shall include a Consent Calendar on the 
9Daily Calendar and designate it as a separate calendar. The 
10Consent Calendar shall contain 3 orders of business: Consent 
11Calendar - Second Reading, Consent Calendar - Third Reading, 
12and Consent Calendar - Resolutions. Within each order of 
13business, bills or resolutions shall be listed in separate 
14groups according to the number of required days each has been 
15on that order of business on the Consent Calendar. No more than 
1625 80 bills and resolutions shall be listed in each group. All 
17bills or resolutions to which amendments have been adopted 
18shall be so designated.
19    (b) No debate is in order regarding any item on the Consent 
20Calendar. The Presiding Officer, however, shall allow a 
21reasonable time for questions from the floor and answers to 
22those questions. No amendment from the floor is in order 
23regarding any bill or resolution on the Consent Calendar.

1    (c) A bill on the Consent Calendar shall stand for 2 
2legislative days on the order of Consent Calendar - Second 
3Reading, and for at least 2 legislative days on the order of 
4Consent Calendar - Third Reading, before a vote on the final 
5passage may be taken. Resolutions on the Consent Calendar 
6shall stand for at least 4 legislative days before a vote on 
7adoption may be taken. One record vote on final passage shall 
8be taken on those bills called for final passage. Immediately 
9before a vote on the bills on the Consent Calendar, the 
10Presiding Officer shall call to the attention of the members 
11the fact that the next legislative action will be the vote on 
12the Consent Calendar.
13    (d) A bill or resolution may be placed on the Consent 
14Calendar by report of a standing committee or special 
15committee upon a motion made only for that purpose that is 
16adopted by a unanimous vote of the members present. For 
17purposes of this subsection (d), a unanimous vote on the 
18motion is a vote with no member voting nay.
19    (e) No bill regarding revenue or appropriations may be 
20placed on the Consent Calendar. No resolution requiring more 
21than 60 affirmative votes for adoption and no bill requiring 
22more than 60 affirmative votes for passage by the House may be 
23placed on the Consent Calendar.
24    (f) The Speaker and the Minority Leader shall each appoint 
253 members who may challenge the presence of any bill or 
26resolution on the Consent Calendar. Before a vote on final

2removed from the Consent Calendar if (i) 4 or more members,

11or resolution on the Consent Calendar.
12    Any bill removed from the Consent Calendar shall stand on 
13the order of Second Reading with short debate status, subject 
14to Rule 52, and any resolution so removed shall stand on the 
15order of Resolutions with short debate status, subject to Rule 
1652.
17    (f-5) Any deadline established under Rule 9 for Third 
18Reading and passage shall not apply to bills that remain 
19pending on the Consent Calendar on the date of such deadline. 
20    (g) This Rule shall not be in effect unless ordered by the 
21Speaker and may be suspended at any time by order of the 
22Speaker. 
23(Source: H.R. 36, 103rd G.A.)
