7    Sec. 10-25. Women, Infants, and Children Nutrition 
8Program. 
9    (a) The Department shall participate in the Women, Infants 
10and Children Nutrition program of the federal government to 
11the maximum extent permitted by the federal appropriation and 
12allocation to the State of Illinois. In order to efficiently 
13process electronically issued WIC benefits, the Department may 
14use an account held outside of the state treasury for the 
15deposit and issuance of WIC benefits. Other appropriations and 
16funds from any public or private source in addition to federal 
17funds may be used by the Department for the purpose of maximum 
18participation in the WIC nutrition program.
19    (b) The Department shall maintain a drug abuse education 
20program for participants in the Women, Infants and Children 
21Nutrition Program. The program shall include but need not be 
22limited to (1) the provision of information concerning the 
23dangers of drug abuse and (2) the referral of participants who

6Program maintained by the Department of Public Health under 
7Section 2310-435 of the Department of Public Health Powers and

17Program in amounts determined by the Secretary.

1    (e) The Department may include a program of lactation 
2support services as part of the benefits and services provided 
3for pregnant and breast feeding participants in the Women, 
4Infants and Children Nutrition Program. The program may 
5include payment for breast pumps, breast shields, or any 
6supply deemed essential for the successful maintenance of 
7lactation, as well as lactation specialists who are registered 
8nurses, licensed dietitians, or persons who have successfully 
9completed a lactation management training program. 
10    (f) The Department shall coordinate the operation of the 
11Women, Infants and Children program with the Medicaid program 
12by interagency agreement whereby each program provides 
13information about the services offered by the other to 
14applicants for services.
15    (g) Subject to appropriation, the Department shall, by 
16rule, expand the WIC Farmers' Market Nutrition Program across 
17the State through local WIC agency grants and contracting 
18local farmers to ensure access to fresh, locally grown fruits 
19and vegetables. The Department shall determine which counties 
20can participate based on funds available and whether there is 
21a sufficient number of farmers willing to accept WIC Farmers' 
22Market Nutrition Program benefits. The Department shall adopt 
23rules to implement this subsection.
