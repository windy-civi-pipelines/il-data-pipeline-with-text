and 6    WHEREAS, Accessible and reliable transportation is 7essential for individuals with ID/DD to live independently, 8access healthcare, pursue education and employment 9opportunities, and fully participate in their communities; and 14    WHEREAS, These transportation providers often face funding 15challenges, operational barriers, and resource limitations 16that hinder their ability to adequately serve the ID/DD 17community; and 18    WHEREAS, Proper support for these providers is necessary 19to maintain and expand transportation services that enable 20individuals with ID/DD to live their fullest lives; and   HR0164- 2 -LRB104 12531 LAW 22959 r1    WHEREAS, The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and 2other federal and State laws require that transportation 3systems accommodate individuals with disabilities, yet ongoing 4challenges persist in ensuring that public and private 5transportation options fully meet the needs of the ID/DD 6community; and 7    WHEREAS, Investments in accessible transit infrastructure, 8paratransit services, and innovative mobility solutions will 9enhance the quality of life for individuals with ID/DD and 10promote their independence; and 11    WHEREAS, Transportation planning and funding decisions 12must incorporate comprehensive input from the ID/DD community, 13advocates, service providers, and other stakeholders to ensure 14equitable outcomes; therefore, be it 15    RESOLVED, BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE 16HUNDRED FOURTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ILLINOIS, THE 17SENATE CONCURRING HEREIN, that any transportation legislative 18package considered by the Illinois General Assembly should 19include complete consideration of the needs of the individuals 20with intellectual and developmental disabilities (ID/DD) 21community, including, but not limited to, accessibility, 22affordability, availability of transportation options, and the 23needs of providers who support individuals with ID/DD; and be   HR0164- 3 -LRB104 12531 LAW 22959 r1it further 2    RESOLVED, That we urge State agencies, regional planning 3organizations, and transportation providers to prioritize 4accessibility, stakeholder engagement, and equitable 5investment in transportation infrastructure to better serve 6individuals with ID/DD;
