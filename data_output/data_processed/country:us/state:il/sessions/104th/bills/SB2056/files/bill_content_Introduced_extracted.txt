The Illinois Dental Practice Act is amended by 5changing Section 17.2 as follows: 6    (225 ILCS 25/17.2)7    (Section scheduled to be repealed on January 1, 2026)8    Sec. 17.2. Teledentistry.9    (a) As used in this Section, "patient of record" means a 10patient for whom the patient's most recent Illinois-licensed 11dentist has obtained a relevant medical and dental history and 12on whom the dentist has (i) performed a physical examination 13within the last year; (ii) obtained relevant records that are 14appropriate for the type of teledentistry service being 15provided from an in-person examination within the previous 12 16months, including a review of the patient's most recent 17x-rays; or (iii) established a relationship with the patient 18through an exchange of protected health information for the 19purpose of providing emergency care, treatment, or services in 20accordance with subsection (c).21    (b) A dentist may only practice or utilize teledentistry 22on a patient of record. A dentist practicing dentistry through 23teledentistry is subject to the same standard of care and   SB2056- 2 -LRB104 10654 AAS 20731 b1practice standards that are applicable to dental services 2provided in a clinic or office setting. A dentist may provide 3and delegate dental services using teledentistry only under 4the supervision requirements as specified in this Act for 5in-person care. Prior to providing teledentistry services to a 6patient, a dentist must obtain informed consent from the 7patient as to the treatment proposed to be offered through 8teledentistry by the dentist. A dentist providing 9teledentistry under this Section shall provide the patient 10with the his or her name, direct telephone number, and 11physical practice address. It is a violation of this Act for a 12provider of dental services rendering care through 13teledentistry to require a patient to sign an agreement that 14limits in any way the patient's ability to write a review of 15services received or file a complaint with the Department or 16other regulatory agency. The Department shall adopt rules to 17provide for the use of teledentistry in the State of Illinois.18    (c) A dentist may treat a patient of record to provide 19emergent care or conduct an initial consultation using 20teledentistry for the purpose of treating or assessing for 21acute pain, infection, injury, or any intraoral or perioral 22condition that presents immediate harm or discomfort to the 23patient for which treatment cannot be postponed. A provider of 24dental services rendering emergent care or conducting an 25initial consultation through teledentistry must direct the 26patient to receive appropriate in-person care after the   SB2056- 3 -LRB104 10654 AAS 20731 b1provision of teledentistry services. 2    (d) A patient of record who is provided services through a 3mobile dental van or portable dental unit, as defined in 4Section 4, does not need to receive an in-person physical 5examination from a dentist prior to receiving treatment that 6utilizes teledentistry if such services are limited to the 7diagnostic, preventive, and periodontal procedures permitted 8within this Act. 9(Source: P.A. 103-902, eff. 8-9-24; revised 10-21-24.)
