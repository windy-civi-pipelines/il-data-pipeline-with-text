14of cycles, including instruction in the safe on-road operation 
15of cycles, the rules of the road and the laws of this State 
16relating to motor vehicles, which courses meet the minimum 
17requirements of this Act, the standards established in the 
18model national administrative standards for state motorcycle 
19rider training programs by the National Highway Traffic Safety 
20Administration, and the rules and regulations issued hereunder 
21by the Department and which have been approved or denied 
22within 60 days by the Department as meeting such requirements.

3    Sec. 2.03a. Cycle Rider Safety Training Course Provider. 
4"Cycle Rider Safety Training Course Provider" and "provider" 
5means a community college, State university, State or local 
6government agency, or for-profit or nonprofit business entity 
7in good standing and operating in the State that is capable of 
8providing courses meeting the definition in this Act in 
9accordance with the rules set forth by the Department and the 
10regulations of this Act. "Cycle Rider Safety Training Course 
11Provider" and "provider" does not include any business 
12registered as a motorcycle dealer with the Secretary of State 
13or any other business that derives income from the selling of 
14motorcycles or has motorcycles for sale at its place of 
15business on a consignment basis.

18    (a) The Department shall, on an as needed basis, put out 
19notices to the public seeking Cycle Rider Safety Training 
20Course Providers to provide courses in this State. Such 
21courses shall be open to all residents of the State who hold a 
22currently valid driver's license and who have reached their 
2316th birthday before the first day of the course to be held. 
24Such courses may be offered throughout the calendar year.
