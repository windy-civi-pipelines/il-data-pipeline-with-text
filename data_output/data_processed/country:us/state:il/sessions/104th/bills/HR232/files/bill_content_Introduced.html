HOUSE RESOLUTION

 
2    WHEREAS, Spondyloarthritis is the name given to a family 
3of closely related, chronic rheumatic diseases that pose a 
4major health concern in the United States, impacting one in 
5100 Americans; and
 
6    WHEREAS, Spondyloarthritis affects 3.2 million adults in 
7the U.S., more than RA, ALS, and MS combined; it can occur 
8early in life and is a condition for which there is no known 
9cure; and
 
10    WHEREAS, Many women, especially Black women, impacted by 
11the disease remain undiagnosed due to inadequate awareness, 
12with the average diagnostic delay being seven to 10 years; and
 
13    WHEREAS, There are fewer than 400 pediatric 
14rheumatologists in the U.S., and approximately one-fourth of 
15the 300,000 children with arthritis must travel 80 or more 
16miles to see a rheumatologist; and
 
17    WHEREAS, Spondyloarthritis includes ankylosing 
18spondylitis, psoriatic arthritis, reactive arthritis, 
19enteropathic arthritis, juvenile spondyloarthritis, 
20undifferentiated spondyloarthritis, peripheral 
21spondyloarthritis, axial spondyloarthritis, and

1non-radiographic axial spondyloarthritis; and
 
2    WHEREAS, Symptoms of spondyloarthritis typically begin 
3before the age of 45, with early signs including frequent pain 
4and stiffness in the lower back and buttocks and pain in a 
5peripheral joint, such as the hip, ankle, elbow, heel, or 
6shoulder; and
 
7    WHEREAS, A portion of people with spondyloarthritis will 
8progress to spinal fusion and will become prone to fractures, 
9thoracic kyphosis, or eroded peripheral joints, especially the 
10hips and shoulders; and
 
11    WHEREAS, Complications from spondyloarthritis may cause 
12serious inflammation in other organs, including the heart, 
13lungs, skin, gastrointestinal tract, nerves, and eyes; and
 
14    WHEREAS, Early diagnosis is critical in effectively 
15treating the disease, preventing certain complications, 
16reducing medical disability, and improving quality of life and 
17function for those impacted with spondyloarthritis; and
 
18    WHEREAS, An increase in awareness of spondyloarthritis and 
19its symptoms, with both medical professionals and the general 
20public, can increase the likelihood of earlier diagnosis with 
21all of its benefits to patients and society; and
