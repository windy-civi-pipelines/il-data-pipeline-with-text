2    WHEREAS, The members of the Illinois House of 
3Representatives are saddened to learn of the death of former 
4State Representative Cynthia "Cindy" Soto, who passed away on 
5March 19, 2025; and
 
6    WHEREAS, Rep. Soto was born to Lydia Niera and Ignacio 
7Sanchez in San Antonio, Texas on July 8, 1962; she moved to 
8Chicago, Illinois at the age of five; and
 
9    WHEREAS, Rep. Soto, while working as a supervisor in the 
10Child Support Division of the Cook County State's Attorney's 
11Office, learned that she needed to address the issues facing 
12her community as an elected official; and
 
13    WHEREAS, Rep. Soto, as a founding member and co-chair of 
14the Latino Caucus and the first Latina to represent the 
15Northwest Side of Chicago, celebrated the vibrant Latino 
16communities of the State and helped pass the TVDL, TRUST and 
17DREAM Acts, ensuring more immigrants can live freely in the 
18State they love and more children can go to college; and
 
19    WHEREAS, Rep. Soto often expressed that two of her 
20proudest legislative achievements were passing the Living Wage 
21Act to raise the State minimum wage and expanding access to
