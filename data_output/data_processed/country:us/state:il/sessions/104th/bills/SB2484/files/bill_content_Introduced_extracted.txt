Full Text of SB2484 ILGA.GOV SB2484 - 104th General Assembly 104TH GENERAL ASSEMBLY State of Illinois 2025 and 2026SB2484 Introduced 2/7/2025, by Sen. Michael W. Halpin SYNOPSIS AS INTRODUCED: 20 ILCS 2705/2705-630 new    Amends the Department of Transportation Law of the Civil Administrative Code of Illinois. Requires the Department of Transportation, in consultation and collaboration with the Department of Central Management Services and the Capital Development Board, to develop one or more standards for State purchases of appliances, concrete, asphalt, steel, and other building materials, subject to appropriation or the award of grant funding for this purpose. Provides that in developing these standards, the establishment of a maximum acceptable Global Warming Potential standard, as well as ways to promote and facilitate the use of life cycle assessments and environmental product declarations, shall be considered when considering bids for State-funded infrastructure projects.LRB104 10873 LNS 20955 b  A BILL FOR SB2484LRB104 10873 LNS 20955 b1    AN ACT concerning State government. 2    Be it enacted by the People of the State of Illinois, 3represented in the General Assembly: 4    Section 5. The Department of Transportation Law of the 5Civil Administrative Code of Illinois is amended by adding 6Section 2705-630 as follows: 7    (20 ILCS 2705/2705-630 new)8    Sec. 2705-630. Sustainability standards. To reduce the 9carbon intensity and increase the sustainability of 10State-funded infrastructure projects, the Department of 11Transportation, in consultation and collaboration with the 12Department of Central Management Services and the Capital 13Development Board, shall develop one or more standards for 14State purchases of appliances, concrete, asphalt, steel, and 15other building materials, subject to appropriation or the 16award of grant funding for this purpose. In developing these 17standards, the establishment of a maximum acceptable Global 18Warming Potential standard, as well as ways to promote and 19facilitate the use of life cycle assessments and environmental 20product declarations, shall be considered when considering 21bids for State-funded infrastructure projects.
