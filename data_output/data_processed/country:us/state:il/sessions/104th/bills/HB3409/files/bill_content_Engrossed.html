1    AN ACT concerning health.
 
2    Be it enacted by the People of the State of Illinois, 
3represented in the General Assembly:
 
4    Section 1. Short title. This Act may be cited as the 
5Chemicals in Cosmetic Products Act.
 
6    Section 5. Purpose. The Act is intended to prohibit, 
7except under certain circumstances, a person from knowingly 
8manufacturing, selling, delivering, holding, or offering for 
9sale in the State a cosmetic product that contains certain 
10ingredients. This Act is intended to be consistent with the 
11prohibition on the presence of the same intentionally added 
12ingredients in cosmetics enacted by the European Union under 
13European Parliament and Council Regulation No. 1223/2009 and 
14subsequent amendments.
 
15    Section 10. Definitions. In this Act:
16    "Chemical Abstracts Service number" or "CAS no." means a 
17unique numerical identifier assigned to a chemical substance 
18by the Chemical Abstracts Service, a division of the American 
19Chemical Society, allowing for precise identification of a 
20specific chemical regardless of its different names or forms. 
21    "Cosmetic product" means a substance or mixture that is 
22intended to be applied to the human body to clean, change its

1appearance, or protect it. "Cosmetic product" includes makeup, 
2hair products, nail products, soaps and lotions, tanning 
3products, and perfumes and eau de cologne. "Cosmetic product" 
4includes the definition of "cosmetic" in 21 U.S.C. 321. 
5    "Ingredient" means any single chemical entity or mixture 
6used as a component in the manufacture of a cosmetic product. 
7"Ingredient" does not include an incidental ingredient, as 
8described in 21 CFR 701.3(l).
