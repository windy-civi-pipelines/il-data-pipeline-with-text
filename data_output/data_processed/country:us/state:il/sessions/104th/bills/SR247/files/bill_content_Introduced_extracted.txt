and 4    WHEREAS, Marjorie Logman was born to Bert and Ann Johnson 5in Batavia on June 10, 1948; she graduated from Madonna High 6School in 1966, and she attended Creighton University from 71966 to 1970; she married Edward Logman in 1970; and 8    WHEREAS, Marjorie Logman taught kindergarten for several 9years, and she took great pride and joy in hearing from her 10former students; and 11    WHEREAS, Marjorie Logman enjoyed all aspects of music, 12whether she was listening, playing the piano, playing the 13autoharp, or singing; she was an intellectually curious person 14and an avid reader and took many classes in wide-ranging 15subject matters; and 16    WHEREAS, Marjorie Logman served as a member and former 17treasurer of the Batavia Historical Society, and she assisted 18in the efforts to create a historical archive for Madonna High 19School; she greatly enjoyed researching, organizing, and 20creating historical articles; and   SR0247- 2 -LRB104 13320 ECR 25599 r1    WHEREAS, Marjorie Logman was a longtime resident of 2Aurora, where she was highly active in civic affairs and 3politics, including advocating for senior citizens, regularly 4speaking at City Council meetings, and participating in 5drafting legislation to protect seniors; and 6    WHEREAS, Marjorie Logman is survived by her children, 7Jason (Elisabeth) Logman, Aaron Logman, and Sarah Logman, and 8her grandson, Benjamin Logman; therefore, be it 9    RESOLVED, BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTH GENERAL 10ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ILLINOIS, that we mourn the passing of 11Marjorie Logman and extend our sincere condolences to her 12family, friends, and all who knew and loved her; and be it 13further 14    RESOLVED, That a suitable copy of this resolution be 15presented to the family of Marjorie Logman as an expression of 16our deepest sympathy.
