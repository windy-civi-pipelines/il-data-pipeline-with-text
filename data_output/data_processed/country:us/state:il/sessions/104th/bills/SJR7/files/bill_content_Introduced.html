SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION

 
2    WHEREAS, It is highly fitting that the Illinois General 
3Assembly pays honor and respect to veterans who have served 
4their country and the State of Illinois; and
 
5    WHEREAS, Private First Class Ralph E. Snell was a member 
6of the 136th Infantry, 33rd Division of the U.S. Army; he 
7entered into service on April 22, 1942, and he was stationed at 
8various camps in the United States until he went overseas on 
9July 5, 1943; he served in the New Guinea and Philippine 
10campaigns and returned to the United States on November 4, 
111945; he was honorably discharged at Camp Grant on November 
1224, 1945; and
 
13    WHEREAS, On May 6, 1945, the 136th Infantry was met by 
14heavy mortar, machine gun, and rifle fire from the flanks and 
15from caves on the reverse slope; as it reached the crest of the 
16hill, a machine gun on the right flank opened fire and wounded 
17four men; PFC Snell moved forward alone and destroyed the 
18weapon and its crew; he then directed his fellow soldiers in 
19destroying three Japanese strong points; although wounded 
20himself, he led his fellow soldiers forward, secured the enemy 
21positions, captured the hill, and only then allowed himself to 
22be evacuated; and

1    WHEREAS, On June 9, 1946, PFC Snell was awarded the 
2Distinguished Service Cross, the army's second highest award 
3for conspicuous gallantry above and beyond the call of duty; 
4along with the cross, he received the Good Conduct Medal, the 
5American Campaign Ribbon, the Asiatic-Pacific Ribbon with two 
6Bronze Battle Stars, the Philippine Liberation Medal with one 
7Bronze Battle Star, and the Purple Heart; and
 
8    WHEREAS, PFC Snell displayed inspiring leadership, 
9indomitable fighting spirit, and heroic effort despite his 
10wounds; therefore, be it
 
11    RESOLVED, BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTH GENERAL 
12ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ILLINOIS, THE HOUSE OF 
13REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING HEREIN, that we designate the Utica 
14River Bridge as the "Ralph E. Snell Memorial Bridge"; and be it 
15further
 
16    RESOLVED, That the Illinois Department of Transportation 
17is requested to erect at suitable locations, consistent with 
18State and federal regulations, appropriate plaques or signs 
19giving notice of the name "Ralph E. Snell Memorial Bridge"; 
20and be it further
 
21    RESOLVED, That suitable copies of this resolution be
