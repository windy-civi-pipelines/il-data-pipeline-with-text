HOUSE RESOLUTION

 
2    WHEREAS, The Assyrian people, a Semitic community who also 
3identify as Chaldeans and Syriacs, are the indigenous people 
4of Mesopotamia who possess a history dating back to ancient 
5times in what is now modern-day Iraq, Syria, Turkey, and Iran; 
6and
 
7    WHEREAS, The Assyrian people have faced systematic 
8persecution in their ancestral homeland, including the 
9Assyrian Genocide of 1915, which claimed the lives of hundreds 
10of thousands, and the Semele Massacre of 1933, where thousands 
11were killed by the Iraqi army; and
 
12    WHEREAS, The Assyrian community in the United States 
13numbers nearly half a million and includes approximately 
14100,000 individuals residing in Illinois, comprising the 
15largest concentration in the nation; and
 
16    WHEREAS, The Assyrian community has shared their rich 
17culture and traditions with neighbors and friends while also 
18serving as model citizens and public servants; and
 
19    WHEREAS, The Assyrian people have made significant 
20contributions to Illinois and the United States through their 
21achievements in the arts, business, media, law, politics,

3    WHEREAS, The Assyrian New Year, known in ancient times as

5and symbolizes rebirth, renewal, and the enduring spirit of 
6the Assyrian people; and

8commemorate Assyrian New Year 6775 with a vibrant parade on 
9Honorary King Sargon Boulevard, along a stretch of Western 
10Avenue, on April 6, 2025; therefore, be it
 
11    RESOLVED, BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE
