6    WHEREAS, OKAW, a landowners' association in southwest 
7Illinois, was formed as a forum for community discussion of 
8landowner issues and a grass-roots planning commission in 
91995, becoming a 501(c)(3) private land trust in partnership 
10with the Illinois Department of Natural Resources' Ecosystems 
11Program of Conservation 2000 (IDNR C2000), which provides 
12financial incentives for landowners to sign conservation 
13easements with the association; and
 
14    WHEREAS, OKAW's purpose is to develop, enhance, and 
15protect the natural resources in the Mid-Kaskaskia River 
16watershed, an important ecosystem whose natural resources 
17include five of the State's largest forest tracts, upland post 
18oak flatwoods, agricultural lands, and coal and oil deposits, 
19due to the area exhibiting apparent herbicide vapor-drift 
20damage and severe bank erosion, among other issues, in recent 
21years; and
 
22    WHEREAS, The Mid-Kaskaskia River watershed's unchannelized
