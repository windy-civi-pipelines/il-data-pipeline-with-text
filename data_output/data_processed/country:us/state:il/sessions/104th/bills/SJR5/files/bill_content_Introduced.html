SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION

 
2    WHEREAS, It is highly fitting that the Illinois General 
3Assembly pays honor and respect to individuals who have given 
4their lives in the line of duty; and
 
5    WHEREAS, Marshal Enoch T. Hopkins was born in Jefferson, 
6Maine on March 22, 1824; he began his long career as a law 
7enforcer shortly after coming to Morris, Illinois in the 
8summer of 1854; and
 
9    WHEREAS, Marshal Hopkins' honorable service was tragically 
10cut short when he was killed in the line of duty on September 
1114, 1878; and
 
12    WHEREAS, On the night of September 14, Frank Foster and 
13Charles Miller, notorious roughs, came to town, where the pair 
14shortly became intoxicated; and
 
15    WHEREAS, Marshal Hopkins advised the men several times 
16over the course of the evening to go home, as they had become 
17disorderly; the pair ignored the multiple warnings they 
18received and persisted with their drinking until late into the 
19evening; and
 
20    WHEREAS, Around midnight, Marshal Hopkins found it

1necessary to arrest the two intoxicated men and caught them in 
2a downtown alleyway; as he was leading them out of the alley, 
3the men fled on foot; and
 
4    WHEREAS, Marshal Hopkins pursued the men when Charles 
5Miller suddenly drew his revolver and fired twice at the 
6officer, who was hit by one of the shots and died almost 
7instantly; and
 
8    WHEREAS, Marshal Hopkins, whose funeral was the largest 
9seen in Morris at the time, was an honored member of the 
10Masonic Order, a great, noble man, and a respected citizen of 
11the community; therefore, be it
 
12    RESOLVED, BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTH GENERAL 
13ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ILLINOIS, THE HOUSE OF 
14REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING HEREIN, that we designate the 
15portion of Illinois Route 47 over Interstate 80 from Romines 
16Drive to Illinois Route 6 East as the "Marshal Enoch T. Hopkins 
17Memorial Road"; and be it further
 
18    RESOLVED, That the Illinois Department of Transportation 
19is requested to erect at suitable locations, consistent with 
20State and federal regulations, appropriate plaques or signs 
21giving notice of the name "Marshal Enoch T. Hopkins Memorial 
22Road"; and be it further
