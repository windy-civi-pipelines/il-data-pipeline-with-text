As used in this Act:7    "Retainage" means a portion of moneys withheld from the 8payment of a contract for a specified period of time to ensure 9that a contractor or subcontractor completes a construction 10project in the manner specified in the contract. "Retainage" 11does not include moneys withheld due to violations of local, 12State, or federal laws.13    "Satisfactory progress" means that a project is moving 14forward according to the established schedule and budget and 15that the work has been performed in accordance with industry 16standards and without deficiencies.  10400HB1224ham001- 2 -LRB104 06221 HLH 22555 a1    "State agency" has the meaning given to "State agencies" 2in Section 1-7 of the Illinois State Auditing Act. 3    Section 10. Determination of satisfactory progress. 4Subject to the limitations set forth in Section 15, with 5respect to a contract entered into on or after the effective 6date of this Act, a State agency may withhold a portion of the 7moneys from the payment of a contract as retainage if and only 8if the State agency determines that satisfactory progress has 9not been achieved by a contractor or subcontractor during any 10period for which a payment is to be made. Retainage may not be 11used as a substitute for good contract management, and the 12State agency may not withhold funds without cause. 13Determinations to retain and the specific amount to be 14withheld must be made by the State agency on a case-by-case 15basis. These determinations must be based on an assessment of 16past performance and the likelihood that such performance will 17continue. 18    Section 15. Limitations on the amount of retainage.
