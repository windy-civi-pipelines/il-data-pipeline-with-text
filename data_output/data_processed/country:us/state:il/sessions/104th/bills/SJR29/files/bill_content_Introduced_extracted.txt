Full Text of SJR0029 ILGA.GOV SJR0029 - 104th General Assembly SJ0029LRB104 13094 MST 25107 r1SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 2    WHEREAS, It is highly fitting that the Illinois General 3Assembly recognizes the sacrifice of U.S. Army Specialist 4Anthony William "Tony" Gilman and his love for his country; 5and 6    WHEREAS, SPC Gilman, son of Fred and Mary Gilman, was born 7in Jerseyville on January 22, 1976; and 8    WHEREAS, SPC Gilman grew up in Calhoun County and 9graduated from Calhoun High School in 1994; and 10    WHEREAS, SPC Gilman enjoyed hunting and fishing throughout 11Calhoun County and Pike County; and 12    WHEREAS, SPC Gilman decided to enlist in the U.S. Army in 131997, eventually rising to the rank of specialist; and 14    WHEREAS, SPC Gilman lost his life on July 4, 1999 during a 15peacekeeping mission in Kosovo, becoming the first American 16casualty during the peacekeeping mission in Kosovo; and 17    WHEREAS, The County of Calhoun is honoring the life and 18sacrifice of SPC Gilman on Memorial Day 2025; therefore, be it   SJ0029- 2 -LRB104 13094 MST 25107 r1    RESOLVED, BY THE SENATE OF THE ONE HUNDRED FOURTH GENERAL 2ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ILLINOIS, THE HOUSE OF 3REPRESENTATIVES CONCURRING HEREIN, that we designate Illinois 4Route 96 from the City of Mozier to the Pike County line as the 5"Army Specialist Anthony William Gilman Highway"; and be it 6further 7    RESOLVED, That the Illinois Department of Transportation 8is requested to erect, at suitable locations consistent with 9state and federal regulations, appropriate plaques or signs 10giving notice of the name "Army Specialist Anthony William 11Gilman Highway; and be it further 12    RESOLVED, That a suitable copy of this resolution be 13presented to the family of SPC Gilman, the Calhoun County 14Board, and the Secretary of Transportation.
