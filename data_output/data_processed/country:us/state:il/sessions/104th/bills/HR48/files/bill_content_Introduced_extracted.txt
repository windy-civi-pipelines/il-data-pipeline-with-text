and 5    WHEREAS, The law details the procedures that states and 6school districts must undertake to meet their obligations 7under IDEA; and 8    WHEREAS, Under IDEA, if a child is deemed eligible to 9receive special education services, school officials are 10required to, in cooperation with a child's parent or guardian, 11develop an individualized education plan (IEP) for that child; 12and 13    WHEREAS, A child's IEP contains critical details about the 14education that the child will receive, including a statement 15of the child's present levels of academic achievement and 16functional performance, measurable annual goals and a 17description of how a child's progress towards achieving those 18goals will be measured, details of the special education and 19related services that the child will receive, any individual 20accommodations that will be needed to measure the child's 21academic achievement and functional performance, and, for 22special education students generally beginning at the age of   HR0048- 2 -LRB104 08429 ECR 18481 r116, measurable postsecondary goals related to training, 2education, employment, and independent living skills; and 3    WHEREAS, In order to ensure a student's success, it is 4imperative that parents remain actively engaged in the 5development and implementation of a child's IEP; however, due 6to the legal complexities and unfamiliar terms associated with 7the process, some parents may encounter difficulties when 8interacting with school district personnel; and 9    WHEREAS, The parents of the nearly 300,000 Illinois 10students with an IEP serve as advocates for their children, 11and to do so effectively, they need to be well informed about 12the procedures associated with establishing an IEP as well as 13the special education students' and their parents' rights; 14therefore, be it 15    RESOLVED, BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE ONE 16HUNDRED FOURTH GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF ILLINOIS, that 17we declare May 2025 as Individualized Education Plan (IEP) 18Awareness Month in the State of Illinois to increase the 19public's understanding of the development and implementation 20of a student's IEP.
